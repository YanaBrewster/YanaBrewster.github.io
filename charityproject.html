Charity Case Study

The Yoobee assignment was to redesign a responsive website.
An ECO member organisation would like you to undertake a review of their
existing site’s user experience and formulate a plan for a site redesign.

We had 3 weeks to complete the proposal, UX, design and coding process.

Strategy

First I brainstormed some of the potential issues that the user may have when researching the organisation and the current website.

Once I had a good idea of what potential issues there were I proceeded with the ‘how might we?’ exercise to find out how we could remedy potential issues and get new members and more engagement for the charity.

Discovery

Using Google Forms I created a survey titled ‘Joining Charitable Organisations’. After getting the survey proof-read and having it tested by a classmate, I started posting it on social media. The results revealed that women were the target audience (70.3%), who were mostly between 21-40 years old (54%) and 24.3% of earners were on a low income.

I received 37 responses in total.

You can view the survey here.

Analysis

I wrote all interview and survey responses on one post-it note (one per person) and placed them in the following categories and compared them to work out who the main target audience was.

From this exercise I could effectively map out the user’s journey, potential obstacles and the important interactions that need to be intuitive in order to have a user-friendly design.

Design

I created a style guide based on the colors and fonts of the main website and campaign site. For the two fonts, I selected a Google serif and sansserif font alternative, each of which load faster on the page. I also found web-safe versions of the colours they use for their branding.

User Testing

Usability Testing (low, medium and high fidelity prototypes) was completed on InVision across the 3 viewing platforms: Desktop, mobile and tablet. Prototypes were created using Sketch and Adobe XD.
